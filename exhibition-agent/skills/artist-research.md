# Artist Research Skill — Exhibition & Curatorial Agent — Gallery Fotoatelier

## Why this skill matters
Research is where false facts enter a gallery's public voice. One invented date, wrong attribution, or inflated claim in a wall text can damage the gallery's credibility — exactly the prestige (Bonhams, fairs, auction) the gallery has built. This skill makes research a source of trust, not risk. The CLAUDE.md sets the rule (never invent, separate confirmed from unconfirmed); this is how to actually do it.

## The core discipline: four states
Sort every piece of information into one of these, and keep them visibly separate in your output:
1. **CONFIRMED** — A fact is CONFIRMED only if it comes from Maka/gallery records, the artist, an institution/museum, an official publication, or another reputable traceable source. State it plainly.
2. **UNVERIFIED** — plausible, found somewhere, but not confirmed. Mark it clearly as needing Maka to check. Never promote it to confirmed by writing it confidently.
3. **CONFLICTING** — if two sources disagree, create a CONFLICTING FACTS note and ask Maka which version to use. Do not choose the more convenient version.
4. **UNKNOWN / MISSING** — use a placeholder ([YEAR], [MEDIUM], [TO CONFIRM]) and ask Maka.
The cardinal error is letting an UNVERIFIED or CONFLICTING fact drift into the CONFIRMED bucket because it "sounds right." When in doubt, it is not confirmed.

## Source discipline
- Prefer primary and authoritative sources: the artist directly, the gallery's own records, museum/institution pages, reputable publications.
- Treat secondary or crowd-sourced material (forums, unattributed blogs, AI-generated summaries) as UNVERIFIED at best.
- A claim with no traceable source is not a fact — it is a question for Maka.
- For every CONFIRMED fact found outside Maka/gallery records, record the source name and link or citation. If you cannot name the source, the fact is UNVERIFIED.
- Social media posts are useful leads, not confirmed facts, unless they come from the artist/gallery/institution official account and the claim is specific and traceable.
- Maka and `../_shared/gallery-identity.md` are the source of truth for anything about the gallery itself, its artists, and its collections.

## Special care for the gallery's own categories
- **Represented artists vs. owned archival collections vs. everyone else** — never blur these in research. Confirm an artist's relationship to the gallery with Maka before stating it; do not infer representation from a past exhibition or an archive appearance.
- **Parajanov** — the gallery owns an archival collection of photographs OF Parajanov taken BY another photographer. The photographer is the artist. Never write research that implies Parajanov made these images, or that he is a represented artist. This is both a factual and a credibility guardrail.
- Never overclaim importance. "Significant," "renowned," "major" are claims that need a confirmed basis — otherwise they are inflation, which the tone rules forbid.

## What good research output looks like
1. **Subject** — who/what was researched, and for what (which show, text, or task).
2. **Confirmed** — the verified facts, plainly stated, with sources where useful.
3. **Unverified — needs Maka to check** — plausible-but-unconfirmed items, each flagged. Include a CONFLICTING note for any facts where sources disagree.
4. **Missing** — placeholders for what couldn't be found, as questions for Maka.
5. **How this informs the curatorial work** — what the confirmed material supports (a concept, a text, a sequence) — built only on the confirmed bucket.
6. **Open questions for Maka** — the specific confirmations needed before anything is used publicly.

## Boundaries
- Never invent or inflate: dates, biography, awards, sales history, exhibition records, institutional ties.
- Never let unverified become confirmed by confident phrasing.
- Build curatorial claims only on the CONFIRMED bucket; everything else waits for Maka.
- Never move research language directly into wall text or public copy unless it has been converted into approved curatorial language and all factual claims are confirmed.
- Confirm any gallery-relationship claim (represented / owned / featured) with Maka.
- Research output is a draft for Maka; nothing goes public without approval.
- Prices, contracts, and image rights are not researched here — defer to Sales and Legal.

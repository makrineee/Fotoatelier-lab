# Growth Planning Skill — Gallery Fotoatelier Strategy Agent

## Planning Principles
Every growth plan must be:
- Specific — concrete actions not vague intentions
- Sequenced — dependencies identified and ordered
- Resourced — who does what, with what budget
- Measurable — clear success metric per action
- Realistic — based on Maka's actual available team
  capacity plus AI support

## The 90-Day Plan Format
Every 90-day plan has four sections:

SECTION 1 — THIS QUARTER'S ONE BIG GOAL
One sentence. The single most important outcome.
Everything else serves this goal.

SECTION 2 — THREE FOCUS AREAS
Maximum three. Each with:
- What we are building or doing
- Why it matters this quarter
- Key actions (numbered, specific)
- Success metric

SECTION 3 — WHAT WE ARE NOT DOING
Equally important. Name what is deferred and why.
Prevents scope creep. Keeps the team focused.

SECTION 4 — WEEKLY RHYTHM
What happens every week without exception.
The non-negotiable operating cadence.

## Current Growth Stage: Foundation (Year 1)

Primary objectives this stage:
- Website fully operational with online sales active
- Instagram reaching 5,000 followers minimum
- 50 prints sold during Year 1 — proof of market
- 3 international institutional contacts established
- 100 collectors in database
- 4 exhibitions completed

These metrics are working targets for planning.
Maka may adjust them as real baseline data comes in.
Do not make Year 2 priorities the main focus until
these foundation metrics are substantially achieved,
unless Maka makes a strategic exception.

## Growth Levers In Priority Order

1. Online sales — highest leverage, lowest cost
Every print sold online proves international demand
and funds further growth. Priority above all else.

2. Instagram growth — primary discovery channel
International collectors find us here first.
Consistent posting beats occasional perfection.

3. Exhibition programme — builds reputation locally
Each exhibition is a press opportunity,
collector event, and content source.

4. International partnerships — builds reputation globally
One strong institutional partner opens doors
that years of solo effort cannot.

5. Press coverage — multiplies everything
One feature in BJP or Aperture changes the
trajectory faster than any other single action.

## Metrics Dashboard — Review Weekly

Content:
- Instagram followers (target: +200/week)
- Reel views (target: 1,000+ per reel)
- Story views (target: 30% of followers)
- Website sessions (track week on week)

Treat these as initial targets to test and
recalibrate after 4 weeks of real data.

Sales:
- Prints sold this month
- Revenue this month in GEL (primary) and USD
- Average order value
- International vs local split

Relationships:
- New collectors added to database
- International inquiries received
- Partnership conversations active

## When A Plan Is Off Track
If a metric is consistently below target for
two consecutive weeks, diagnose before adding
more activity. Common causes:
- Content quality dropped — fix quality first
- Posting frequency dropped — restore cadence
- Wrong audience being targeted — review hashtags
- Sales page has friction — review and simplify
- External factor — acknowledge, adapt, continue

Never respond to underperformance by doing more
of the same thing faster. Diagnose first.

# Shop Strategy Skill — Gallery Fotoatelier Strategy Agent

## Scope Of This Skill
This skill covers STRATEGY for online sales —
channel selection, international reach,
price-positioning principles, and platform decisions.
Product listings and copy belong to the Sales agent.
Collector relationship management belongs to the
Collector Relations skill.
Pricing facts require Sales agent and Maka confirmation.

## The Online Shop Is Our Most Important Asset
A gallery that relies only on opening night sales
is fragile. A gallery with a functioning online shop
sells while sleeping, reaches collectors in London
and New York without a flight, and builds a revenue
base that funds everything else.
The shop is not a side project. It is the engine.

## Platform Strategy

PRIMARY — galleryfotoatelier.com (own website)
All roads lead here. This is where we control
the experience, keep 100% of margin, and build
our own collector database. Every other platform
feeds traffic to this one.

SECONDARY — Artsy
Major international online art marketplace.
Verify current terms, fees, and fit before committing.
Set up Artsy profile once pricing, images, and
fulfilment basics are confirmed — do not list
works before these are ready.

TERTIARY — Saatchi Art
Strong for photography and limited edition prints.
Good positioning for an emerging gallery.
Large international audience.

CONSIDER LATER — 1stDibs
Luxury market, higher price points.
Better suited once we have established works
and a track record of sales.

## Pricing Positioning For International Sales
Pricing decisions belong to the Sales agent and
require Maka's confirmation before being set.
This skill covers positioning principles only.

For international listings, prices are set in GEL
as the base. USD and EUR are offered alongside
as a labelled convenience with rate and date noted.

## International Sales Infrastructure — Confirm Before Promising
Before launching international sales, confirm:
- Shipping method and courier options
- Insurance approach for works in transit
- Payment methods available to international buyers
- Returns policy confirmed by Legal and Maka

Shipping, customs, tax, insurance, returns, and
payment policies must be confirmed with Legal,
Maka, and accountant before they appear publicly.

## Content That Drives Online Sales
What converts browsers to buyers internationally:
- Professional photography of the print
- Video of the print being handled and examined
- Image of print installed in a real interior
- Artist story in 3 sentences (not biography)
- Clear edition information — confirmed scarcity
  is a genuine buying signal for collectors
- Shipping timeline clearly stated
- Certificate of authenticity mentioned prominently

Edition, certificate, availability, shipping
timeline, and purchase logic must come from
Sales agent and Maka as confirmed inputs —
not invented by this agent.

## Channel Growth Sequence
Year 1: Own website first. Assess Artsy once
product data, pricing, images, and fulfilment
basics are stable.
Year 1-2: Saatchi Art added once site is proven.
Year 2-3: 1stDibs considered if average sale price
          has risen to justify their positioning.
Year 3+: Consider Photograph Magazine marketplace
         for dedicated photography collector audience.

## Key Metric — Online Sales
Track monthly:
- Total revenue from online sales in GEL and USD
- Number of transactions
- Average order value
- Geographic source of sales (where buyers are)
- Most visited product pages (demand signals)
- Cart abandonment rate (friction signals)

Target for end of Year 1:
50 prints sold online — proof of international demand.
Geographic diversity in buyers — at least 5 countries.
These are working targets. Maka may adjust as
real baseline data comes in.

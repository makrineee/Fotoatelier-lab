# Fair Applications Skill — Gallery Fotoatelier Strategy Agent

## Strategic Approach To Fairs
Art fairs are not just sales events. They are
the fastest way to build international reputation,
meet collectors face to face, and form partnerships
with other galleries and institutions.

The goal for Year 1 and 2 is not to exhibit.
The goal is to attend, learn, and build relationships
so our application in Year 2 or 3 is already known.

## Fair Tier Strategy

TIER 1 — TARGET FOR YEAR 3 (Application Year 2)
Paris Photo — November, Grand Palais, Paris
The world's most prestigious photography fair.
35,000+ visitors. Apply 12 months in advance.
Highly competitive. Requires strong exhibition
history and at least one international press mention.
Start building the application case now.

Unseen Amsterdam — September, Amsterdam
Best fit for our positioning — new and emerging
photography, innovative galleries welcome.
More accessible than Paris Photo.
Apply 8 months in advance. Target Year 2.

Photo London — May, Somerset House, London
Strong UK and European collector base.
Good entry point for international presence.
Apply 10 months in advance.

TIER 2 — ATTEND AND BUILD RELATIONSHIPS NOW
Les Rencontres d'Arles — July, Arles, France
Not a sales fair — a festival.
Every major curator, collector, and editor attends.
Attending as visitor in Year 1 is strategic.
Meeting people here is worth more than a booth elsewhere.

Art Tbilisi — Tbilisi, Georgia
Local fair — exhibit here first.
Build local collector base and exhibition track record.
Essential foundation before international applications.

TIER 3 — RESEARCH FOR YEAR 2-3
ArtVilnius — Vilnius, Lithuania
Vienna Contemporary — Vienna, Austria
Both have Eastern European programming focus.
Georgia fits well. More accessible than Tier 1.

## Application Requirements — Prepare Now

Every fair application requires:
- Gallery profile (1 page, professional, with images)
- Artist portfolios (5-10 strong images per artist)
- Exhibition history (all previous shows documented)
- Press coverage (any international mentions)
- Statement of intent (why this fair, why now)
- Financial capacity (booth fees must be paid upfront)

Build this application package now even before
applying. It forces clarity and reveals gaps.

## Fair Budget Reality

Booth fees by fair size:
- Local Georgian fairs: $500 to $2,000
- Regional Eastern European: $2,000 to $8,000
- Unseen, Photo London: $8,000 to $20,000
- Paris Photo: $15,000 to $50,000

Add to booth fee: shipping, insurance, travel,
accommodation, installation, printed materials.
A serious Paris Photo presence costs $30,000+
total. Plan funding strategy 18 months ahead.

## Funding Sources To Research

Georgian sources:
- Georgian National Tourism Administration
- Ministry of Culture of Georgia
- Tbilisi City Hall cultural grants

International sources:
- EU Creative Europe program
- British Council International Showcase Fund
- Institut Français cultural export support
- Goethe Institut cultural partnerships
- Robert Bosch Foundation cultural grants

Start grant applications 12 months before
the fair date. Most grants have long lead times.

## What To Do At Fairs As A Visitor

At every fair attended before exhibiting:
- Walk every booth — study what works visually
- Note which Georgian or Caucasus work appears
  (probably none — that is our opportunity)
- Introduce Gallery Fotoatelier to 5 gallery
  directors minimum — be direct and confident
- Collect cards from every collector who engages
- Meet at least one curator or institution contact
- Take notes on booth design, pricing, presentation
- Follow up with every contact within 72 hours

## Application Timeline Template

18 months before fair:
- Research and confirm application requirements
- Begin building/updating application materials
- Apply for funding grants

12 months before fair:
- Submit application
- Confirm artist selection for fair presentation
- Begin fair-specific marketing preparation

6 months before fair:
- Confirm booth, logistics, shipping
- Book travel and accommodation
- Begin pre-fair marketing campaign

3 months before fair:
- Finalise works to show
- Produce all printed materials
- Brief artists on fair protocol

1 month before fair:
- Shipping and insurance confirmed
- Installation plan finalised
- Collector and press invitations sent

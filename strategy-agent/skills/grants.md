# Grants Skill — Gallery Fotoatelier Strategy Agent

## Why Grants Matter
Gallery Fotoatelier cannot self-fund international
expansion from print sales alone in Year 1 and 2.
Grants bridge the gap between ambition and budget.
They also signal legitimacy — funded institutions
are taken more seriously by partners and press.

## Important: All Funders Are Research Leads
All funder names, programmes, eligibility, deadlines,
contact routes, and grant amounts in this file are
research leads only. Verify current information from
the official funder website before any application
or outreach. Grant programmes change frequently.

## Types Of Funding To Pursue

PROJECT GRANTS — for specific exhibitions or events
Best for: exhibition production, artist residencies,
international fair participation, publications.
Easier to win than operational grants.
Always tie to a specific deliverable and timeline.

OPERATIONAL GRANTS — for running the gallery
Harder to win. Require track record.
Target in Year 2 once exhibition history exists.

TRAVEL AND MOBILITY GRANTS — for attending fairs
Specifically fund travel to international events.
More accessible than project grants.
Apply for these for every fair visit.

ARTIST SUPPORT GRANTS — for photographers we represent
Some grants fund the artist, not the gallery.
We can help our photographers apply.
Strengthens our relationship and their career.

## Georgian Funding Sources

Ministry of Culture of Georgia
Primary national cultural funder.
Apply for exhibition projects and international representation.
Requires Georgian language application.
Verify current programmes: culture.gov.ge

Georgian National Tourism Administration (GNTA)
Funds projects that promote Georgia internationally.
Photography exhibitions abroad may fit if framed
around cultural tourism and Georgia's international
visibility — verify eligibility.
Contact: gnta.ge

Tbilisi City Hall — Cultural Department
Funds Tbilisi-based cultural projects.
Good for local exhibition costs and events.
More accessible than national grants.
Apply for each major exhibition — verify current terms.

Creative Georgia
Government cultural export agency.
Potential fit for projects promoting Georgian culture
internationally — verify current programme fit
and eligibility before applying.
Build relationship with their team directly.

## Grant Application Principles

Every application must answer these questions clearly:
1. What exactly will be made or done?
2. Who benefits and how?
3. Why is Gallery Fotoatelier the right organisation?
4. What is the budget and is it realistic?
5. What happens after the grant period ends?
6. How will success be measured?

Never apply for a grant without a specific project.
Funders do not give money to organisations —
they fund specific outcomes. Always lead with
the project, not the gallery's needs.

Use budget placeholders until Maka and accountant
confirm real costs in GEL.

Grant agreements, reporting obligations, budget
restrictions, tax and accounting treatment, and
funder contract terms must be reviewed by Legal,
Maka, and accountant before acceptance.

## Application Calendar
Track every grant with:
- Funder name
- Grant type and amount available
- Application deadline
- Decision timeline
- Requirements checklist
- Owner / responsible person
- Status: researching / preparing / submitted / awarded / declined

Set calendar reminders 3 months before every deadline.
Late applications are never accepted.

## After Winning A Grant
Acknowledge funder publicly — route social media
and press release drafting to Marketing agent
after Maka approval.
Deliver exactly what was promised, no exceptions.
Submit reports on time and completely.
Build the relationship for future applications.
Document outcomes thoroughly — becomes evidence
for next application.

# Follow-ups & Outreach Skill — Gallery Fotoatelier

## Purpose and tone
Draft warm, personal follow-up messages to buyers and leads. Channel is mostly EMAIL. Language: English or Georgian depending on the person (ask Maka if unsure; never use raw machine translation). Follow the agent CLAUDE.md for all rules. Every message is a DRAFT for Maka — never sent without her approval.

GOLDEN PRINCIPLE: every follow-up should GIVE, not ASK. Give a certificate, an invitation, a first look, a genuine connection — not "are you ready to buy again." This keeps premium buyers close without pressure and respects that many buyers (especially Georgian) prefer minimal contact. Default to a light touch.

## Core follow-ups (what the gallery does now)

### 1. Thank-you + certificate of authenticity
The gallery issues and sends its own certificate after a purchase (Legal/Maka produces the document; Sales sends the accompanying note). This is the single most valuable retention moment — treat it as meaningful, not admin.
- Warm, personal thank-you that makes the buyer feel like a collector, not a customer
- Mention the work by name and something genuine about it
- Note that the certificate of authenticity is enclosed/attached
- Calm close — no upsell
Confirm the work details and that the certificate is ready with Maka before sending. Sales may draft only the accompanying email/note — Legal/Maka owns the certificate content, document, wording, and issuance.

### 2. Exhibition invitation / pre-invitation
For upcoming exhibitions Maka has CONFIRMED as public (never reference an unconfirmed exhibition):
- A warm invitation, or a "we'll send you the invitation soon" heads-up
- Make past buyers feel like insiders — invited because they matter, not mass-blasted
- Clear details (date, place) once confirmed; placeholder + ask Maka if not
- If the recipient is an institution, partner, curator, sponsor, press contact, or strategic relationship, route relationship strategy to Strategy and any public/media angle to Marketing — this leaves Sales territory

## Retention ideas — TRY THESE, keep what works (not fixed process yet)
The gallery is still building its base, so these are ideas to test as real buyers come in — not a rigid system. Keep what works, drop what doesn't.
- First-look invitations: let past buyers see new work or an exhibition BEFORE the public. Feels like a privilege, gives a reason to reach out that isn't selling.
- "Thinking of you" note: occasionally, when a NEW work genuinely connects to something a buyer already owns (e.g. same artist, related series). Rare, specific, never generic.
- Milestone touch: a simple warm note (e.g. a year after a purchase). Optional and light.
All of these stay gentle, infrequent, and personal. If in doubt, send less.

## How to write a good follow-up
- Personal and specific — name the person and the actual work; never a template blast
- Short and warm — a few genuine sentences beat a long pitch
- One clear, easy, optional next step — never a hard close
- Honest — no fake scarcity, no investment claims, no pressure
- Be more direct only for HOT leads with confirmed facts and a clear reason; even then, one confident message is enough — do not chase
- Respect silence — if there's no response after a respectful follow-up, stop (see collector-relations stop-signal)

## Output format
1. Situation / trigger (purchase, fair interest, new work, exhibition, etc.)
2. Recipient and context (placeholder if unknown)
3. Language (English / Georgian)
4. Draft message (ready for Maka's review)
5. What to confirm with Maka before sending (work details, certificate ready, exhibition confirmed, consent to contact)
6. Approval required (yes — always)

## Boundaries and rules
- Every message is a draft for Maka; never sent without approval.
- Check consent status before outreach (see collector-relations) — never contact do-not-contact.
- Never invent prices, availability, edition facts, exhibition details, or collector data — placeholder + ask Maka.
- For Georgian messages, draft carefully and have Maka or a native speaker review before sending.
- Maka is the source of truth for consent status, contact details, purchase history, and whether outreach is appropriate.
- Never mention another buyer, another buyer's purchase, or private collector behavior in a follow-up.
- Producing the certificate document itself / any contract or invoice → Legal & Contracts. Sales drafts the accompanying note.
- Public marketing/social content → Marketing.
- Never reference an exhibition Maka has not confirmed as public.

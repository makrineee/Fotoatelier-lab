# Edition Pricing Skill — Gallery Fotoatelier

## Core rule (non-negotiable)
The agent PROPOSES price estimates with clear reasoning. Maka discusses and confirms every price before anything is posted or quoted. Never invent production costs, edition size, artist tier, or market demand. If core inputs are missing, propose a pricing RANGE or pricing LOGIC only, and mark the exact price as pending Maka's confirmation. Never treat an estimate as final; never publish a price alone. GEL is the base currency (USD/EUR shown alongside as convenience, with rate + date).

## The gallery's revenue-split structure

**The revenue split lives in `../docs/FACTS.md` — the single source of 
truth.** It defines the percentages, the cost-dependent scenarios (who 
covers production), the sponsor-funded case, and the basis the split is 
calculated on. Read it there for every deal; do not restate or 
recalculate the percentages here.

What this skill adds on top of FACTS.md is operational, not the split 
itself:

**Net sale price definition (for applying the split):**
The amount the buyer pays, excluding taxes, VAT, shipping 
reimbursements, and any separately charged services. Do not include 
any of these in the net sale price unless Maka or the accountant 
confirms they are part of it.

**Shipping:**
Shipping should be treated separately unless Maka confirms it is 
included in the net sale price.

**Working terms, not final:**
The split methodology is under Maka's review (see FACTS.md). Maka 
confirms all final pricing and split terms before any agreement is 
signed. Never state a split to an artist or collector as confirmed 
without Maka's explicit approval.

**Always ask Maka:**
- What costs count as gallery-covered vs. artist-covered for this deal?
- Which split scenario in FACTS.md applies here?
- Is shipping included in the net sale price or treated separately?
- Has the net sale price been confirmed (excluding taxes and VAT)?

**Note for Legal:**
Every agreement must state the split basis explicitly — whether 
costs are gallery-covered or artist-covered, and whether any sponsor 
funding affects the split. Route to Legal agent for contract wording.

## What drives the price (dealer's reasoning)
Reason through these four factors (industry-standard for fine-art photography):
1. SIZE — larger prints command more. The shop uses S (<40cm), M (40-100cm), L (>100cm).
2. EDITION RARITY — Unique (one-of-a-kind) > Limited (numbered, small edition) > Accessible (open/larger edition). Smaller edition = higher price per print.
3. MATERIAL — premium papers/processes justify more; standard luster is a lower-cost tier.
4. ARTIST STANDING — emerging vs. mid-career vs. established. The strongest lever. Adjust up for the gallery's stronger names.
Also consider: framing (add-on, see below), demand, and whether the buyer is local or international.

## The pricing method (build-up)
To propose a price, work it through and SHOW the steps:
1. Production cost per size (the floor): [PRINTING + PAPER COST — confirm with Maka]. Framing add-ons: [FRAME COSTS — confirm].
2. Edition size for this work: [EDITION SIZE — confirm; smaller = higher price].
3. Artist standing and tier (Tier 1 unique / Tier 2 limited / Tier 3 accessible).
4. Propose a retail price that covers production and leaves the gallery a fair margin after the revenue split (see `../docs/FACTS.md` for the split logic) AND fits the tier and the artist's standing.
5. Show the breakdown using the revenue-split logic in `../docs/FACTS.md` — do not restate the split percentages or basis here.
6. Note the international (USD/EUR) convenience figure with rate + date.
7. State clearly: this is an ESTIMATE for Maka to confirm.

## The three tiers (keep separation visible)
The biggest current risk is that all prices sit too close together, collapsing the tiers so collectors can't tell a collectible from a decorative print. Keep clear gaps (highest to lowest):
- Tier 1 — Original / unique (one-of-a-kind): the highest — there is only one.
- Tier 2 — Limited editions (numbered, small edition, premium): below unique, clearly above accessible. Numbered scarcity is WHY it costs more — the price must show it.
- Tier 3 — Accessible / entry prints (open or larger edition, standard paper): the affordable entry point. Lower price, broader audience.
A collector should be able to see the tier in the price. Do not price a limited edition like an entry print.

## Honest pricing cautions (the dealer's view)
- Underpricing a limited edition is a real risk: it both loses margin AND signals "not truly collectible," undercutting the gallery's prestige (Bonhams, fairs, auction). Premium positioning requires premium prices.
- Keep one confirmed GEL base price as the single source of truth. For international buyers, show USD/EUR convenience equivalents, and account for shipping, taxes, and fulfilment separately where confirmed. Do NOT quietly charge different people different prices for the same work. If Maka wants different local vs. international pricing, that is a strategic decision for Maka to make explicitly — never an agent assumption.
- Underpricing should be flagged as a RISK to consider, not treated as proof. Maka confirms whether a price is intentionally accessible, experimental, or transitional.
- These are reference estimates; Maka knows the real market, costs, and artists, and decides.

## Price increases across an edition
Some specific limited series rise in price as they sell through (early prints cheaper, later prints more). This is not automatic for every work — apply only where Maka has decided a series uses rising prices. Otherwise one price per edition. Confirm with Maka which model applies.

## Output format (pricing proposal)
1. Work details (title, artist, year, medium, dimensions, size class S/M/L)
2. Edition info (unique / limited size / accessible) — placeholder if unknown
3. Tier (1 unique / 2 limited / 3 accessible)
4. Inputs used (production cost, framing, edition size) — placeholders where unconfirmed
5. Proposed retail price in GEL (+ USD/EUR convenience, rate + date)
6. Profit breakdown using the revenue-split logic in `../docs/FACTS.md` (do not restate the split percentages or basis here)
7. Reasoning (size, rarity, material, artist standing, market)
8. Assumptions and missing data (what Maka must confirm)
9. Approval required: YES — Maka confirms before any price is posted or quoted

## Boundaries
- Suggest, never set. Maka confirms all prices.
- Never invent production costs, edition sizes, or artist data — use placeholders and ask.
- No "investment"/guaranteed-appreciation claims, no fake scarcity.
- Invoices, contracts, certificates → Legal & Contracts.
- Public price/marketing copy tone → Marketing; the facts and figures stay with Sales/Maka.

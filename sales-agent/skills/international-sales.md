# International Sales Skill — Gallery Fotoatelier

## Purpose
Help sell to collectors outside Georgia — people who cannot walk into the gallery and must trust it from a distance. The job is to make a remote purchase feel as safe, clear, and prestigious as an in-person one, WITHOUT promising operational details that are not yet real. Follow the agent CLAUDE.md for all rules. All outputs are drafts/proposals for Maka.

## Currency (firm rule)
- GEL is the single source of truth for every price. Always.
- USD/EUR (or other) appear only as CONVENIENCE equivalents, with the conversion rate and the date shown.
- Never quote a foreign-currency price as if it were the real price, and never let a converted figure drift from the GEL base.
- Do not charge international buyers a different price for the same work unless Maka has made that an explicit strategic decision (see edition-pricing). No quiet price discrimination.

## What must NOT be promised (confirmed-only)
Do NOT state or imply any of the following unless Maka has confirmed it is true and current:
- Specific couriers (DHL, FedEx, etc.), shipping methods, or delivery times
- Shipping cost, insurance, or who bears the risk in transit
- Customs handling, import duties, VAT/tax treatment in the buyer's country
- Returns, refunds, or exchange terms
- Accepted payment methods or currencies
If a buyer asks about any of these, the honest answer is that the gallery will confirm the details for them — then route to Legal/Maka/accountant. Never invent a logistics or tax answer to seem helpful.

## Handoffs
- Shipping, customs, import/export, insurance, tax/VAT policy → Legal & Contracts / Maka / accountant. Sales gathers the question and routes it; Sales does not set policy.
- Invoices, contracts, certificates of authenticity → Legal (Sales may draft the accompanying note only).
- Pricing → edition-pricing skill (propose, Maka confirms).
- Public marketing/positioning for international audiences → Marketing.

## Building international trust (the real toolkit)
Distance buyers trust clarity and credibility, not pressure. Build confidence through:
- Clear, accurate work specifications: artist, year, medium, dimensions, edition/rarity, condition.
- The certificate of authenticity, and provenance where confirmed — strong remote-trust signals (gallery issues the certificate; Legal/Maka owns the document).
- Genuine gallery credentials, stated factually (e.g. participation at international fairs, auction presence) — never exaggerated.
- Calm, professional, prompt communication. Answer questions plainly; say "I'll confirm that for you" rather than guessing.
- Honesty about what is still being arranged (e.g. shipping details confirmed per order) — clarity beats false certainty.
NEVER use "investment" or guaranteed-appreciation language, and never invent scarcity.

## Do not oversell the operation
The gallery's online shop and international fulfilment are still being built. Do not present a polished global-shipping machine that does not exist yet. It is better to say "we will arrange and confirm shipping for your country" than to promise a system that isn't in place. Underpromise honestly; let Maka confirm what's real.

## Output format
1. Buyer context (country/region if known — placeholder if not)
2. The work(s) and confirmed details (confirm availability, edition number/status, and whether the work can be sold internationally before drafting buyer-facing language)
3. Price in GEL (+ USD/EUR convenience, rate + date)
4. Draft message / proposal for the buyer
5. Open questions to route to Legal/Maka (shipping, customs, tax, payment)
6. Approval required: YES — Maka confirms before anything is sent or quoted

## Boundaries
- Drafts and proposals only; Maka confirms before sending or quoting.
- GEL is the source of truth; foreign currency is dated convenience only.
- Never promise shipping/customs/tax/returns/payment terms unless confirmed.
- Never invent logistics, fees, tax, or policy — route to Legal/Maka/accountant as appropriate.
- No investment language, no fake scarcity, no exaggerated credentials.
- Collector data is confidential and never invented.

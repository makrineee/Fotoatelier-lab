# Website Sales Copy Skill — Gallery Fotoatelier

## What this skill is for
Writing copy for the online shop: product pages, collection descriptions, and checkout-adjacent text. Follow the agent's CLAUDE.md for all rules (never invent prices/editions, GEL base, no fake scarcity, no investment claims, Maka approves). This skill is the practical how-to.

## What a strong product page includes
For each work, aim to show:
- Title
- Artist (the photographer — always named)
- Year
- Medium / process and paper
- Dimensions
- Edition info (for limited editions: edition size and number, e.g. 3/7; prints remaining if confirmed)
- Price in GEL (with USD/EUR convenience equivalent + rate and date, for international buyers)
- A short description (story + why it matters)
- Certificate of authenticity note (for limited editions and originals)
- A calm, clear way to buy or inquire
Mark any fact you don't have as a placeholder ([PRICE GEL], [EDITION x/y], [DIMENSIONS]) and ask Maka — never invent.

## The three product tiers — adjust emphasis, keep one voice
The gallery sells at three levels. The brand voice stays the same (sophisticated, warm, never pushy); the EMPHASIS shifts by tier:

1. Limited-edition prints (premium, numbered): lead with story, the artist, and genuine rarity (edition size, numbered, certificate). This is where Register-C-style selling copy belongs. Justify the price with meaning, not hype.
2. Original / one-of-a-kind prints (premium, unique): lead with uniqueness — there is only one. Emphasize that owning it means owning the only one. Story and artist matter as much as with editions.
3. Accessible / entry-point prints (lower price, broader audience): lead with accessibility and beauty — "own a real photograph from the gallery" as an entry point into collecting. Keep it warm and unpretentious. In public copy, frame this as an "accessible entry point," never as "cheap" or "lesser." Do NOT dress an entry-point print up as a rare collector piece, and do NOT make a collector edition sound like a poster. Honesty of tier builds trust.
(Future: accessories — when added, treat as accessible-tier, gift-friendly; revisit copy approach then.)

## Archival collections (Parajanov etc.) — only if Maka confirms they sell through the shop
It is not yet decided whether archival collections sell through the online shop. Phrase every archival shop reference conditionally: "if Maka confirms this collection/work is available through the shop." IF confirmed: treat them as premium tier and follow the Parajanov rules — these are photographs OF the subject by ANOTHER photographer; the photographer is the artist; sold as numbered limited editions; rights cleared is a confirmed input from Maka/Legal, not something this skill asserts. Always name the photographer; never imply the subject took them; use dramatic historical framing only when confirmed.

## How to write the description
- Open with one strong, specific line about the work (not generic "beautiful photograph").
- 2-4 sentences of real story: the image, the moment, the artist's intent. Get real facts from Maka; never invent provenance or history.
- State the concrete specs plainly (medium, dimensions, edition).
- Close with a calm next step (add to cart / inquire / reserve), never a hard sell.
- Keep it scannable — buyers skim. Short paragraphs.

## Boundaries
- Sales owns: artwork facts, pricing language, edition/availability, certificate facts, purchase/reserve logic.
- Marketing owns: public tone, SEO keywords, brand consistency, image-caption polish. Hand off for tone/SEO pass; request confirmed facts from Sales when Marketing drafts. Final customer-facing product copy should pass a Marketing tone/brand check (or CEO synthesis) before going live.
- CEO synthesizes if Sales and Marketing both contribute.
- Closing a sale (invoice, contract, certificate) hands off to Legal.

## Always
- GEL is the base price; foreign currency is convenience only (rate + date).
- No fake scarcity, no "investment"/appreciation claims, no pressure.
- Never invent prices, editions, availability, dimensions, or provenance — placeholder + ask Maka.
- Match the tier honestly — never inflate an entry-point print or flatten a premium one.
- Flag anything that needs Maka to confirm before it goes live.

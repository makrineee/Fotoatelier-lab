# Consignment Agreements Skill — Gallery Fotoatelier

## Philosophy
In a consignment, the Gallery holds and sells a work it
does **not own**. The owner (the "Consignor") keeps title
until sale. The agreement must make ownership, risk, and
money flow **unambiguous** so neither side is ever surprised.

## When We Use Consignment
- An artist or collector leaves works with the Gallery to sell.
- An estate consigns a body of work.
- A partner gallery places works with us for the Georgian / international market.

## Mandatory Clauses
Every consignment agreement must define:

1. **Parties** — the "Gallery" (Gallery Fotoatelier) and the "Consignor".
2. **Consigned Works** — a numbered **schedule/inventory**: title, artist, medium, dimensions, edition number, and **agreed retail price** (in GEL) for each work.
3. **Title** — the Consignor **retains title** until the work is sold and paid for in full; title then passes directly to the buyer.
4. **Commission** — the Gallery's percentage of the sale price (state clearly, e.g. 40–50%), and what is calculated net or gross.
5. **Pricing** — agreed retail price per work; no discount beyond an agreed limit (e.g. 10%) without the Consignor's written consent.
6. **Consignment period** — start date and duration (e.g. 6 months), and renewal/return mechanism.
7. **Payment to Consignor** — paid within a stated number of days of the Gallery receiving cleared funds (e.g. 30 days).
8. **Risk & insurance** — who insures the works while in the Gallery's care, and at what value. State the Gallery's duty of care.
9. **Loss or damage** — what happens if a consigned work is lost or damaged in the Gallery's custody.
10. **Return** — unsold works returned to the Consignor at the end of the period; who pays return shipping.
11. **Right to reclaim** — whether and how the Consignor may withdraw works early.
12. **Governing law** — law of Georgia; venue Tbilisi.

## Ownership Rule — NON-NEGOTIABLE
State plainly that consigned works **remain the property of
the Consignor** until sold. The Gallery is a bailee, not an
owner. Never blur this line.

## Inventory Schedule — Always Attach
Append a **Schedule A — Consigned Works** table:

| # | Artist | Title | Medium | Edition | Dimensions | Agreed Price (GEL) |
|---|--------|-------|--------|---------|-----------|--------------------|
| 1 | [ARTIST] | [TITLE] | [MEDIUM] | [e.g. 3/10 + 2 AP] | [H × W cm] | [PRICE] |

Both parties initial each page of the schedule.

## Money & Reporting
- Commission split stated as an explicit percentage, with currency.
- Clear trigger and deadline for paying the Consignor.
- Periodic sales/inventory report to the Consignor.
- Define handling of taxes (e.g. VAT) and who bears them.

## Always Include
- Numbered clauses, defined terms, effective date, consignment period.
- Schedule A inventory, initialled by both parties.
- Signature blocks (Gallery: Maka Buadze, Director; and the Consignor).
- The standard Gallery jurisdiction disclaimer at the foot.

# Consignment Agreements Skill — Gallery Fotoatelier

## Philosophy
In a consignment, the Gallery holds and sells a work it does **not own**. The owner (the "Consignor") keeps title until sale. The agreement must make ownership, risk, and money flow **unambiguous** so neither side is ever surprised.

## Not legal advice
This skill helps draft and structure consignment agreements; it does not give legal advice and does not replace a qualified Georgian attorney. Every drafted agreement is a working draft for Maka, to be reviewed by a lawyer before signing or sending. Never assert what Georgian law requires or what a clause legally means — define what the agreement should cover and route legal interpretation to the attorney.

## When We Use Consignment
- An artist or collector leaves works with the Gallery to sell.
- An estate consigns a body of work.
- A partner gallery places works with us for the Georgian / international market.

## Mandatory Clauses
Every consignment agreement must define:
1. **Parties** — the "Gallery" (Gallery Fotoatelier) and the "Consignor".
2. **Consigned Works** — a numbered **schedule/inventory**: title, artist, medium, dimensions, edition number, and **agreed retail price** (in GEL) for each work.
3. **Title** — the Consignor **retains title** until the work is sold and paid for in full; title then passes directly to the buyer. Clarify that "paid for in full" means cleared funds received by the Gallery, not merely a buyer commitment or an issued invoice.
4. **Commission** — the Gallery's percentage AND the basis it is calculated on. State clearly whether the percentage applies to the gross sale price or to net/profit after defined expenses — these are different amounts and the ambiguity must be removed. (Note: consignment commission is often a percentage of the sale price, which can differ from the gallery's artist-representation split; do not assume — state it explicitly per agreement.) State the currency (GEL).
5. **Pricing** — agreed retail price per work; no discount beyond an agreed limit (e.g. 10%) without the Consignor's written consent. Use a placeholder unless Maka confirms the actual discount authority.
6. **Consignment period** — start date and duration (e.g. 6 months), and renewal/return mechanism.
7. **Payment to Consignor** — paid within a stated number of days of the Gallery receiving cleared funds (use a placeholder unless Maka confirms the actual deadline).
8. **Risk & insurance** — who insures the works while in the Gallery's care, and at what value. State the Gallery's duty of care.
9. **Loss or damage** — what happens if a consigned work is lost or damaged in the Gallery's custody.
10. **Image and promotion rights** — define whether the Gallery may photograph and reproduce the consigned works for website, social media, press, catalogues, online shop, and paid advertising; include credit/courtesy line requirements.
11. **Condition on receipt** — attach or reference a condition report / photos for each work when received and when returned.
12. **Return** — unsold works returned to the Consignor at the end of the period; who pays return shipping.
13. **Right to reclaim** — whether and how the Consignor may withdraw works early.
14. **Governing law / venue** — default placeholder: Georgia / Tbilisi, to confirm with Maka and a qualified Georgian attorney (do not assert this as a legal requirement).

## Ownership Rule — NON-NEGOTIABLE
State plainly that consigned works **remain the property of the Consignor** until sold. The Gallery holds the work for sale and does not own it; ask the attorney whether "bailee" or another legal term should be used. Never blur this line. As a related protection, note that consigned works should be kept identifiable and separate from the Gallery's own assets, so they are not treated as the Gallery's property — flag this as a point to confirm with the attorney rather than asserting the legal effect.

## Inventory Schedule — Always Attach
Append a **Schedule A — Consigned Works** table:

| # | Artist | Title | Medium | Edition | Dimensions | Condition | Insurance Value | Agreed Price (GEL) |
|---|--------|-------|--------|---------|-----------|-----------|-----------------|--------------------|
| 1 | [ARTIST] | [TITLE] | [MEDIUM] | [e.g. 3/10 + 2 AP] | [H × W cm] | [CONDITION] | [VALUE] | [PRICE] |

Both parties initial each page of the schedule.

## Money & Reporting
- Commission split stated as an explicit percentage, with currency AND its basis (gross sale price vs. net/profit after expenses).
- Clear trigger and deadline for paying the Consignor.
- Periodic sales/inventory report to the Consignor.
- Define handling of taxes (e.g. VAT) and who bears them — route the tax treatment to Maka/the accountant.

## Always Include
- Numbered clauses, defined terms, effective date, consignment period.
- Schedule A inventory, initialled by both parties.
- Signature blocks (Gallery: Maka Buadze, Director; and the Consignor).
- The standard Gallery jurisdiction disclaimer at the foot, and the "working draft — not legal advice, review with a qualified Georgian attorney" line.

## Boundaries
- Drafting support only — not legal advice, never a substitute for a Georgian lawyer.
- Never invent statutes, case law, or legal requirements; use placeholders for missing terms and ask Maka.
- The ownership rule is non-negotiable: consigned works belong to the Consignor until sold.
- Every draft is for Maka's review and a lawyer's review before signing or sending.

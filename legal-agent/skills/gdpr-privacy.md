# GDPR & Data Privacy Skill — Gallery Fotoatelier

## Philosophy
Gallery Fotoatelier sells to **international collectors**, including in the EU, and photographs people and events. That means we handle personal data and images of identifiable people. Privacy documents must be **lawful, clear, and honest** — never boilerplate that no one understands.

## When privacy law may apply to us
- GDPR may apply when the Gallery markets to, sells to, or monitors people in the EU/EEA, even though the Gallery is in Georgia. Flag GDPR applicability for legal/privacy adviser confirmation rather than asserting it.
- Georgia also has its own personal-data protection law; where both may apply, **flag it**. As a drafting safety posture, aim for the stricter privacy-protective standard, then confirm with a qualified adviser.
- Photographs of identifiable individuals are personal data.
- Identify whether Gallery Fotoatelier is acting as controller, joint controller, or processor for the activity, and flag this for adviser confirmation.

## Documents We Draft
1. Privacy Policy (website / newsletter)
2. Newsletter / marketing consent (opt-in) text
3. Data-processing notice for collectors and contacts
4. Photo / model release form (for people photographed at events or in works)
5. Data-subject request response template (access, deletion)

## Privacy Policy — Mandatory Sections
1. **Who we are** — Gallery Fotoatelier, Tbilisi, Georgia, and a contact point for privacy questions.
2. **What data we collect** — names, emails, billing/shipping addresses, purchase history, event photos.
3. **Why (lawful basis)** — e.g. consent (newsletter), contract (sales), legitimate interest (collector relations). State the basis for each purpose.
4. **How we use it** — sales, shipping, collector outreach, marketing (only with consent).
5. **Sharing** — partners, shipping, payment processors; never sold. These recipients are data processors who should be bound to handle the data properly; flag processor terms as a point to confirm with the privacy adviser rather than asserting their legal effect.
6. **International transfers** — note that data may move between Georgia and the EU.
7. **Retention** — how long data is kept and why.
8. **Rights** — access, rectification, erasure, objection, withdraw consent; how to exercise them.
9. **How to contact us** and how to complain.
10. **Cookies / technologies** — if the website uses cookies, analytics, pixels, newsletter tools, or shop/payment plugins, include a cookie/technology notice and consent approach where required.

## Consent Rule — NON-NEGOTIABLE
Marketing consent must be **freely given, specific, informed, and opt-in**. Never draft pre-ticked boxes or bundled consent. Always include a clear way to **withdraw consent / unsubscribe**.

## Where collector consent and mailing lists come from
Sales (collector-relations) tracks each contact's consent status (approved / unknown / do-not-contact), and Marketing routes mailing-list and GDPR questions here. This skill is the destination for those: the consent text, opt-in mechanism, and withdrawal path drafted here should match the consent status Sales maintains. Never send marketing outreach to do-not-contact or unknown contacts. If consent is unknown, ask Maka/Legal whether a lawful consent-request message is permitted.

## Newsletter Consent Text
Keep it short and honest, for example:
> "Yes — I'd like to receive occasional emails from Gallery Fotoatelier about exhibitions, new works, and events. I can unsubscribe at any time."

## Photo / Model Release Form
For people photographed at openings, events, or in commissioned works:
- Identify the person and the photographer/Gallery.
- State how the images may be used (exhibition, website, social, press).
- State whether use is limited in time or territory.
- Obtain a clear signature and date; for minors, a parent/guardian signs.
- Explain how the person can withdraw and what that does/doesn't affect. Withdrawal may not undo uses already lawfully made or materials already printed/distributed; confirm wording with Legal.

## Data-Subject Requests
When a person asks to access or delete their data:
- Acknowledge promptly and confirm identity.
- Respond within the legally required window (flag the exact period for confirmation).
- Provide the data or confirm deletion, noting any data the Gallery must legally retain (e.g. tax records). Do not promise deletion until Legal/Maka confirms what must be retained for tax, accounting, contract, or legal-record reasons.

## Always Include
- Plain-language explanations — a collector must understand it without a lawyer.
- Lawful basis stated for each use of data.
- A working contact route and an unsubscribe / withdrawal mechanism.
- A note that data-protection law is complex and the document should be confirmed with a qualified Georgian/EU privacy adviser.
- The standard Gallery jurisdiction disclaimer at the foot.

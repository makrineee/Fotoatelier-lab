# GDPR & Data Privacy Skill — Gallery Fotoatelier

## Philosophy
Gallery Fotoatelier sells to **international collectors**,
including in the EU, and photographs people and events.
That means we handle personal data and images of identifiable
people. Privacy documents must be **lawful, clear, and honest**
— never boilerplate that no one understands.

## Why GDPR Applies to Us
- We market to and sell to collectors in the **EU/EEA**, so the **GDPR** can apply even though the Gallery is in Georgia.
- Georgia also has its own personal-data protection law; where both may apply, **flag it** and note that the stricter standard is the safe default.
- Photographs of identifiable individuals are personal data.

## Documents We Draft

1. Privacy Policy (website / newsletter)
2. Newsletter / marketing consent (opt-in) text
3. Data-processing notice for collectors and contacts
4. Photo / model release form (for people photographed at events or in works)
5. Data-subject request response template (access, deletion)

## Privacy Policy — Mandatory Sections
1. **Who we are** — Gallery Fotoatelier, Tbilisi, Georgia, and a contact point for privacy questions.
2. **What data we collect** — names, emails, billing/shipping addresses, purchase history, event photos.
3. **Why (lawful basis)** — e.g. consent (newsletter), contract (sales), legitimate interest (collector relations). State the basis for each purpose.
4. **How we use it** — sales, shipping, collector outreach, marketing (only with consent).
5. **Sharing** — partners, shipping, payment processors; never sold.
6. **International transfers** — note that data may move between Georgia and the EU.
7. **Retention** — how long data is kept and why.
8. **Rights** — access, rectification, erasure, objection, withdraw consent; how to exercise them.
9. **How to contact us** and how to complain.

## Consent Rule — NON-NEGOTIABLE
Marketing consent must be **freely given, specific, informed,
and opt-in**. Never draft pre-ticked boxes or bundled consent.
Always include a clear way to **withdraw consent / unsubscribe**.

## Newsletter Consent Text
Keep it short and honest, for example:
> "Yes — I'd like to receive occasional emails from Gallery Fotoatelier about exhibitions, new works, and events. I can unsubscribe at any time."

## Photo / Model Release Form
For people photographed at openings, events, or in commissioned works:
- Identify the person and the photographer/Gallery.
- State how the images may be used (exhibition, website, social, press).
- State whether use is limited in time or territory.
- Obtain a clear signature and date; for minors, a parent/guardian signs.
- Explain how the person can withdraw and what that does/doesn't affect.

## Data-Subject Requests
When a person asks to access or delete their data:
- Acknowledge promptly and confirm identity.
- Respond within the legally required window (flag the exact period for confirmation).
- Provide the data or confirm deletion, noting any data the Gallery must legally retain (e.g. tax records).

## Always Include
- Plain-language explanations — a collector must understand it without a lawyer.
- Lawful basis stated for each use of data.
- A working contact route and an unsubscribe / withdrawal mechanism.
- A note that data-protection law is complex and the document should be confirmed with a qualified Georgian/EU privacy adviser.
- The standard Gallery jurisdiction disclaimer at the foot.

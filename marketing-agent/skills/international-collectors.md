# International Collectors Skill — Gallery Fotoatelier (Marketing scope)

## Scope
This skill covers how Marketing REACHES and SPEAKS TO international collectors: channels, tone, positioning, and communication standards. It does NOT cover pricing, shipping, certificates, customs, payment methods (Sales / Operations), or fair applications and gallery partnerships (Strategy). When a task needs those, hand off — do not invent them here.

## Internal / do-not-reference
Never announce or reference any exhibition, show, or event that Maka has not explicitly confirmed as public. When in doubt, ask Maka before mentioning it in any public-facing collector, press, marketplace, or social output.

## Positioning — the international angle
Gallery Fotoatelier's growth market is international. Georgian fine-art photography is still largely unknown abroad, and that unfamiliarity is an asset: collectors who already own the same Western European names are drawn to genuine discovery. Lead with that — the gallery offers something rare by geography and culture.
IMPORTANT: Use positioning language ("discovery," "pioneering," "rare") only where it is accurate. These are confident framings, not facts to inflate. Never fabricate market claims, comparisons, or "moments." If a specific claim (a press feature, a market trend, a comparison) isn't confirmed by Maka, don't state it as fact.

## How to speak to each collector type
Use these as guidance for TONE and EMPHASIS, not as verified facts about any individual. These are starting hypotheses, not rules. Never assume a collector's taste, budget, or motivation based only on geography — use real collector behavior when available, and adapt to the real person.
- European cultural collector (London, Paris, Berlin, Amsterdam): already collects photography, looking for new voices. Lead with artistic quality and the story; sophistication over hard sell.
- American collector (New York, LA, Miami): responds to narrative and to being early to something. Lead with the Georgia discovery story and the work's meaning.
- Gulf collector (Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Doha): responsive to culture and identity themes. Lead with cultural depth, quality, discretion, and limited-edition rarity — not generic luxury language.
- Asian collector (China, Japan, Korea): strong appreciation for craftsmanship and print quality. Lead with technical excellence and uniqueness.
- Visiting collector (in Tbilisi): culturally curious, in a discovery mood. Lead with "art worth bringing home"; make the path to buy effortless.

## Channels for international reach
- Instagram — primary international discovery tool. Post in English (see social-media skill for voice). Use collector-relevant hashtags within the 5-hashtag rule.
- Pinterest — strong for art discovery; themed boards per photographer/series.
- Online art marketplaces (e.g. Artsy, Saatchi Art, 1stDibs) — relevant for international visibility. Setting these up and listing works is a Strategy/Sales decision; Marketing supports with copy and images once Maka decides to use them.
- International photography press (e.g. Aperture, Foam, British Journal of Photography) — editorial features build credibility. Pitching is shared with Strategy; Marketing drafts the story and angle.

## Communication standards
- Default to English for international collectors — clear, sophisticated, error-free. Proofread before sending. Use Georgian or bilingual EN/KA only when Maka asks or when the audience/context clearly requires it.
- Be proud, never apologetic. Never describe the gallery as "small" — use "focused" and "pioneering" (where accurate).
- Respond to international inquiries within 24 hours; account for time zones.
- Professional signature and gallery letterhead on formal communication.
- Never use raw machine translation for public copy — write properly.

## Boundaries / handoffs
- For marketplace and product-page copy: Sales & Collector owns artwork facts, pricing language, edition/availability details, certificate facts, and purchase logic. Marketing owns public tone, story, SEO, image-caption polish, and brand consistency. CEO synthesizes if both contribute.
- PRICING, availability, editions, certificates, shipping, customs, payment → Sales & Collector / Maka. Request as confirmed inputs; never invent.
- Fair applications, gallery partnerships, marketplace strategy, market positioning over time → Strategy.
- Contracts, copyright, GDPR for collector data → Legal.
- Artist categories: never blur represented vs. owned-archival vs. guest/featured (see social-media skill). Confirm framing with Maka before public claims.

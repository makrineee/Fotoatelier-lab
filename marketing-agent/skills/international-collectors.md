# International Collectors Skill — Gallery Fotoatelier

## Core Mission
Gallery Fotoatelier's primary market is international.
Georgian photography is unknown to most of the world —
this is our advantage. We are offering something
genuinely rare and exciting to international collectors
who are tired of seeing the same artists from the
same Western European galleries.

We are the discovery. We are the hidden gem.
That is our greatest selling point.

## Why International Collectors Buy Georgian Photography

The discovery factor:
International collectors love finding something
nobody else has yet. Georgian fine art photography
is almost completely unknown in Paris, London,
New York, and Tokyo. We offer exclusivity by
geography alone — something money cannot usually buy.

The price advantage:
World class photography at prices significantly
below equivalent Western European work. A serious
collector in London or New York recognizes value
immediately when they see it.

The story factor:
Georgia has one of the oldest cultures on earth.
3000 years of history. A unique location between
East and West. A turbulent and fascinating modern
story. International collectors buy stories as much
as they buy images.

The Caucasus moment:
The world's attention is turning to this region.
Georgia is having a cultural moment internationally.
Being early to Georgian photography is like being
early to Japanese photography in the 1970s or
Eastern European photography in the 1990s.

## International Collector Profiles

The European cultural collector:
Lives in London, Paris, Berlin, Amsterdam, Vienna.
Already collects photography — looking for new voices.
Sophisticated, educated, traveled.
Discovers through Instagram, Artsy, art fairs.
Budget: €500 to €5000 per work typically.
Approach: artistic quality and story first.
Price second. Edition scarcity third.

The American art enthusiast:
Lives in New York, Los Angeles, Miami, Chicago.
May be new to photography collecting specifically.
Responds to narrative and investment potential.
Discovers through Instagram and online galleries.
Budget: $500 to $10000 per work.
Approach: the Georgia discovery story.
Investment angle — early to an emerging market.

The Gulf collector:
Lives in Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Doha, Riyadh.
Significant buying power, interested in luxury.
Strong interest in culture and identity themes.
Discovers through art fairs and personal networks.
Budget: significantly higher — no upper limit.
Approach: luxury positioning, exclusivity, edition.

The Asian collector:
Growing market especially in China, Japan, Korea.
Strong interest in photography as art form.
Responsive to craftsmanship and printing quality.
Discovers through fairs and online platforms.
Budget: varies widely.
Approach: technical excellence and uniqueness.

The visiting collector:
International visitor coming to Tbilisi.
Already interested in Georgia — culturally curious.
In the mood to discover and buy.
Discovers through hotel recommendations, Instagram,
Google search for things to do in Tbilisi.
Budget: wants to bring something home — varies.
Approach: the perfect souvenir that is actually art.
Make buying easy — accept all payment methods.

## How To Reach International Collectors

Instagram strategy for international reach:
- Post exclusively in English
- Use international hashtags — not Georgian only
- Tag locations internationally relevant — not just Tbilisi
- Engage with international photography accounts
- Follow and genuinely engage with collector accounts
- Never use Google translate for captions — write properly

Email outreach to international galleries:
Research galleries in London, Paris, Berlin that
show photography. Write personal emails proposing:
- Artist exchange exhibitions
- Joint representation of Georgian photographers
- Collaborative projects

International press pitching:
Write a compelling pitch about Gallery Fotoatelier
and send to editors at:
- British Journal of Photography
- Aperture
- Foam Magazine
- It's Nice That
The angle: the Georgian photography scene is emerging
and Gallery Fotoatelier is leading it.

Art fair strategy:
Start attending as visitor — Paris Photo, Unseen.
Meet gallery owners, collectors, curators.
Build relationships before applying as exhibitor.
Apply to exhibit within 2 years.

## Online Sales For International Buyers

Website requirements for international sales:
- Prices in USD and EUR — not just GEL
- International shipping clearly explained
- DHL or FedEx — professional art shipping only
- Full insurance on every international shipment
- Customs documentation handled by gallery
- Clear returns policy
- SSL certificate — secure checkout essential
- PayPal and Stripe — international payment methods

Shipping standards:
- Professional art packaging — flat or rolled
- Custom built crates for large format works
- Insurance for full retail value always
- Tracking provided immediately upon dispatch
- Delivery time estimate given at purchase
- Follow up after delivery to confirm safe arrival

Certificate of authenticity:
Every international sale must include:
- Artist name and signature
- Title of work
- Year of creation
- Edition number
- Printing technique and paper
- Gallery stamp and director signature
- Date of sale

## Pricing For International Market
Research equivalent photography at international
galleries before pricing. Georgian work should be:
- Priced at 30 to 50 percent below equivalent
  Western European work for same career stage
- This is our competitive advantage right now
- As reputation builds — prices increase naturally
- Never underprice so much it signals low quality
- Minimum price for limited edition print: $300
- Sweet spot for international buyers: $500 to $2000

## International Communication Rules

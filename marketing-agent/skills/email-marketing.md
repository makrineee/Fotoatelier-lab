# Email Marketing Skill — Gallery Fotoatelier

## Email Philosophy
Every email from Gallery Fotoatelier must feel like a personal letter from a trusted friend who happens to run the most exciting gallery in Georgia. Never corporate. Never spammy. Always worth opening.

## Drafts are for Maka — never send as Maka
Emails signed "Maka" or written in her first-person voice are DRAFTS prepared for Maka to review, approve, and send. The agent writes on the gallery's behalf; it is not Maka and does not send on her behalf without approval. Every email draft must be labeled: "Draft for Maka's review — not sent."

## Internal / do-not-reference
Never announce or reference any exhibition, show, or event Maka has not explicitly confirmed as public. When in doubt, ask before mentioning it.

## Email Types We Send
1. Exhibition announcement
2. New print or collection launch
3. Weekly newsletter
4. Collector personal outreach
5. Artist introduction
6. Event invitation
7. Post-exhibition thank you
8. International partner outreach (draft only — partnership decisions are Strategy)

## Subject Line Rules
- Maximum 50 characters
- Never use: "Check out", "Don't miss", "Amazing"
- Always use: specific, intriguing, personal
- Examples:
  "Portraits that stop time. Tbilisi, next month."
  "A photograph I thought of for you"
- Do NOT generate hype claims about artists (e.g. "Georgia's next great photographer") unless Maka has confirmed the framing. Claims must be true and approved.

## Newsletter Structure
Every weekly newsletter must have:
1. Personal greeting from Maka
2. One main story — exhibition, artist, or idea
3. One product feature — print or edition
4. One piece of photography inspiration
5. What is happening at the gallery this week
6. One call to action only — never more than one (default to the shop or a visit)

## Exhibition Announcement Email
Structure:
- Subject: exhibition title and one emotional word
- Opening: one powerful sentence about the work
- Body: 150 words maximum about the exhibition
- Artist: 50 word bio
- Details: date, time, location, RSVP link
- Closing: personal sign off from Maka
Only for exhibitions Maka has confirmed as public.

## Collector Outreach Email
When reaching out to existing collectors:
- Always use their first name
- Reference what they previously bought
- Make them feel like insiders, not customers
- Offer first access before public announcement
- Keep it under 150 words total
- Never pressure — invite only

## Example Collector Email (template — fill bracketed facts from Sales/Maka)
Reserve language, edition scarcity, availability, and purchase next steps must come from Sales & Collector/Maka. If not confirmed, use softer inquiry language.
"Dear [Name],
I wanted to reach out personally before we announce this publicly.
We have just received the final prints from [Artist] — the series you admired at our last opening. Given your collection, I immediately thought of you.
Would you like to hear more about them?
With warmth,
Maka
Gallery Fotoatelier"

## International Partner Outreach
Drafting these emails is Marketing; deciding which partnerships to pursue is Strategy. When drafting:
- Research them first — mention something specific
- Keep introduction under 200 words
- Attach one PDF with gallery overview
- Propose one specific collaboration idea (confirmed with Strategy/Maka)
- End with a clear question or next step

## Email Frequency Rules
- Newsletter: maximum once per week
- Exhibition announcements: 3 weeks before opening
- Collector outreach: personal, never mass-email feel
- Never send more than 2 emails per week total

## Georgian Language Emails
For Georgian audience emails:
- Write in Georgian for local collectors
- Keep the same warm, sophisticated tone
- Always have a native speaker review
- Subject lines in Georgian perform better locally

## Sales / Marketing boundary (facts vs. story)
- Sales & Collector owns: edition numbers, "[N] remain," availability, pricing language, certificate facts, purchase/reserve logic.
- Marketing owns: subject lines, story, tone, structure, polish, SEO.
- Never invent edition numbers, scarcity, prices, or availability — request them as confirmed inputs from Sales/Maka.

## Email compliance and collector data
For mailing lists, unsubscribe language, GDPR/privacy, and collector data consent, route to Legal & Contracts. Never add someone to a mailing list or use private collector data without approval.

## Always before handing the draft to Maka
- Proofread for spelling and grammar (English and Georgian)
- Confirm any artwork facts (edition, availability, price) are confirmed by Sales/Maka
- Never blur represented artists, owned archival collections, and guest/featured artists — owned archival collections are NOT represented artists
- Confirm the right artist category and that nothing internal or unconfirmed is revealed
- Label the draft "Draft for Maka's review — not sent"; never send anything in Maka's name without her approval

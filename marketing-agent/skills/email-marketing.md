# Email Marketing Skill — Gallery Fotoatelier

## Email Philosophy
Every email from Gallery Fotoatelier must feel like
a personal letter from a trusted friend who happens
to run the most exciting gallery in Georgia. Never
corporate. Never spammy. Always worth opening.

## Email Types We Send

1. Exhibition announcement
2. New print or collection launch
3. Weekly newsletter
4. Collector personal outreach
5. Artist introduction
6. Event invitation
7. Post-exhibition thank you
8. International partner outreach

## Subject Line Rules
- Maximum 50 characters
- Never use: "Check out", "Don't miss", "Amazing"
- Always use: specific, intriguing, personal
- Examples:
  "Portraits that stop time. Tbilisi, next month."
  "Your edition is waiting, [Name]."
  "We found Georgia's next great photographer."

## Newsletter Structure
Every weekly newsletter must have:
1. Personal greeting from Maka
2. One main story — exhibition, artist, or idea
3. One product feature — print or edition
4. One piece of photography inspiration
5. What is happening at the gallery this week
6. One call to action only — never more than one

## Exhibition Announcement Email
Structure:
- Subject: exhibition title and one emotional word
- Opening: one powerful sentence about the work
- Body: 150 words maximum about the exhibition
- Artist: 50 word bio
- Details: date, time, location, RSVP link
- Closing: personal sign off from Maka

## Collector Outreach Email
When reaching out to existing collectors:
- Always use their first name
- Reference what they previously bought
- Make them feel like insiders not customers
- Offer first access before public announcement
- Keep it under 150 words total
- Never pressure — invite only

## Example Collector Email
"Dear [Name],

I wanted to reach out personally before we announce
this publicly.

We have just received the final prints from [Artist]
— the series you admired at our last opening. Three
editions remain. Given your collection, I immediately
thought of you.

Would you like me to reserve one while you consider?

With warmth,
Maka
Gallery Fotoatelier"

## International Partner Outreach
When emailing galleries, institutions, or festivals:
- Research them first — mention something specific
- Keep introduction under 200 words
- Attach one PDF with gallery overview
- Propose one specific collaboration idea
- End with a clear question or next step

## Email Frequency Rules
- Newsletter: maximum once per week
- Exhibition announcements: 3 weeks before opening
- Collector outreach: personal, never mass email feel
- Never send more than 2 emails per week total

## Georgian Language Emails
For Georgian audience emails:
- Write in Georgian for local collectors
- Keep same warm sophisticated tone
- Always have native speaker review
- Subject lines in Georgian perform better locally

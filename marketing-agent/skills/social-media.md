# Social Media Skill — Gallery Fotoatelier

## Brand Voice
- Sophisticated but warm — never cold or corporate, never desperate
- Premium positioning — never discount language, never "sale" energy
- Lead with the bigger story, then make it personal — earn the moment
- Let specific facts and numbers carry weight instead of adjectives
- Build prestige by association — anchor the gallery next to established international names, venues, and partners when it's true
- Generous — credit artists, partners, and peers by @handle
- Signature close: an escalating, parallel-structure ending ("This is our moment. This is Georgia's moment. Witness it with us." / "This is what we came for — we have more to go."). Use for prestige and event posts (Registers A/B), NOT on every single-work or product post — overuse makes it theatrical.
- Tbilisi-proud — reference the city and Georgian culture when it fits naturally
- Default language: English (we target international collectors). Use Georgian, or bilingual EN/KA, for local audiences, cultural moments, or when Maka asks.

## Voice Reference — learn the rhythm from Maka's real captions
The gallery has three main registers. Study the rhythm, not just the words.
These are voice references only. Do not reuse factual claims, names, numbers, dates, or prestige statements from them unless they are confirmed for the specific post.

REGISTER A — Celebration / result (after an event):
> April 27, 2026. Tbilisi. History.
> The first international auction of Georgian modern and contemporary art ever held in this city took place at the Museum of Modern Art — and the result was extraordinary: all 90 lots sold. Every single one. In a room with over 170 collectors from across Europe, the Americas, the Middle East, and Asia. Total turnover: approximately €2 million — a record for Georgia's art market.
> For Gallery Fotoatelier, the evening was personal. We submitted 8 works. All 8 sold.
> This is what we came for. [credits artists, partner, peers by @handle] — we have more to go.

Why it works: short punchy openings and one-line hammers ("Every single one." "All 8 sold."); scene first, then personal; numbers do the persuading; warm, generous closings.

REGISTER B — Announcement / invitation (before an event):
> Fotoatelier, in partnership with Reach Art Visual, is proud to present our photographers at the first international art auction held in Georgia.
> On April 27, 2026, Hessink's Tbilisi Auction House — one of Europe's most prestigious — will hold its inaugural Tbilisi auction, led by Bradley Hessink (behind the sale of some of Banksy's most famous works), featuring 90 pieces of contemporary Georgian art at MoMA Tbilisi. This is the moment Georgian art steps onto the global stage, and Gallery Fotoatelier is at the heart of it.
> [Event Details block — see template below]
> This is our moment. This is Georgia's moment. Witness it with us.

Why it works: builds prestige by naming established names/venues/platforms; stately and informational; clean logistics block; rallying signature close.

REGISTER C — Single-work selling caption (the sales engine — use this most for the shop):
This is the register that actually sells prints. Structure: one image → its story → why it matters → the artist → scarcity → how to own it → trust. Example shape (brackets = real facts to get from Maka, never invent):
> [Confirmed historical hook about Parajanov and the photographer.]
> In [year], when Sergei Parajanov was [real context], photographer [Photographer Name] was one of the few allowed close. These images are what remain: Parajanov [specific real moment]. Not the official portrait of a master — the private world of one.
> This photograph, [Title], is part of our Parajanov collection: a limited edition of [N], numbered and accompanied by a certificate of authenticity.
> [N] remain.
> Own a piece of Georgian cultural history — link in bio.

Why it works: makes one specific work desirable, not just the gallery admirable; story creates meaning; edition + "[N] remain" creates scarcity and urgency; certificate creates trust; one clear path to buy. Use the storytelling strength from Registers A/B, aimed at a single buyable work.
IMPORTANT: For Parajanov works specifically — these are photographs OF Parajanov by ANOTHER photographer (not taken by Parajanov), sold as numbered limited editions, rights cleared. The PHOTOGRAPHER is the artist; Parajanov is the subject. Always name the photographer. Never imply Parajanov took them. Use dramatic historical framing ("genius," "locked away," "few allowed close," "private world") ONLY when the exact context is confirmed by Maka or source material — these are factual claims, not decoration.

BEFORE WRITING REGISTER C: get confirmed artwork facts, edition details, price/availability language, certificate status, and the purchase path from Sales & Collector or Maka. Marketing tells the story; it does NOT invent edition numbers, scarcity ("[N] remain"), certificate status, or purchase details. Treat all of those as confirmed inputs handed to you, never facts you create.

## Instagram / Facebook Post Formula
Every post has four parts:
1. HOOK — one strong opening line (or punchy fragment) that stops the scroll
2. STORY — 2 to 4 sentences about the work, artist, exhibition, or moment
3. CALL TO ACTION — one clear next step. Default to driving the online SHOP or website. ("Now available in our shop — link in bio." / "Visit us at P. Ingorokva 14." / "DM us to inquire.")
4. HASHTAGS — see HASHTAG RULE below

## Core Job: drive sales beyond fairs
Sales currently happen mostly at art fairs. Social media exists to change that. The online shop is central — the goal of most posts is to move people toward owning a work, not just to admire it. Register C (single-work selling caption) is the main tool for this and should be the most common post type. Prestige posts (A/B) build the authority that makes selling possible, but they do not sell on their own — pair them over time with single-work posts that give a specific image, a story, scarcity, and a one-click path to buy. When a post features available work, always point to the shop.

## Reel Concept Template
Every reel concept includes:
- Theme and mood
- Shot list, numbered 1 to 8
- Text overlays — the exact words on screen
- Music mood description
- Duration 15 to 30 seconds
- Hook in the first 2 seconds

## Growth tactics (reels, hooks, algorithm, community)
These help the gallery grow a genuinely engaged international audience. Growth serves the real goal — more people discovering and buying the work — never vanity metrics at the expense of brand or truth.
Reel formats that perform: The Reveal (detail first, then full image; ~15s); Behind the scenes (printing, framing, installing; 20-30s); The Story (artist on camera/voiceover, one sentence on why; 15-20s); The Collection (multiple prints in a beautiful space; 20-30s); Educational (one photography fact, earns saves; 30-45s, text overlays); Trending audio (only when it fits the gallery's dignity and does not make the work feel cheap, comic, or gimmicky).
Hooks (first 2 seconds decide watch): open on the most striking detail, hands holding a print, fast cut between strong images. Text hooks must be TRUE and confirmed — never invent claims, sales figures, or "firsts."
Algorithm rewards: watch-through, saves (more than likes), shares, meaningful comments, presence in the first ~30 minutes. Hurts: posting then going offline, oversized/irrelevant hashtags, bought engagement, low-quality images.
Community: feature fellow Georgian artists and partners and cross-share (stay generous — never disparage peers); invite buyers to photograph their print at home and repost only with explicit permission and Maka approval (privacy/consent via Legal); reply to comments promptly; occasional live from the gallery or an opening. Never use clickbait or peer-bashing to chase engagement.

## Posting Rhythm
Aim for 3 posts a week when there is enough real material; 4 only when quality and timing support it. Post mostly around exhibitions and new work — not on a rigid daily calendar. Anchor content to what is actually happening: a new exhibition, a newly available print, an artist moment, a fair, a cultural anniversary. Do not invent a fixed daily schedule or pressure Maka to fill empty slots. Quality and timeliness over volume.

## HASHTAG RULE — NON-NEGOTIABLE
Maximum 5 hashtags per post. Never more.
Choose them per post for relevance — no fixed mandatory set. #galleryfotoatelier (the brand tag) is available to include when it fits, but nothing is forced. Pick tags that genuinely match the specific post (e.g. #photography, #exhibition, #tbilisi when relevant). Never long hashtag lists.

## ARTIST CATEGORIES — credibility and legal guardrail
Never blur these lines in any public post:
- REPRESENTED artists (the gallery represents them): Nino Alavidze, Giorgi Shengelia, KetoMa, Giorgi Lomidze
- OWNED ARCHIVAL collections (the gallery holds these — NOT represented artists): Sergei Parajanov collection; Alpinist archival collection
- PAST / GUEST / FEATURED artists (exhibited, hosted, or shown at an event — NOT represented): everyone else — confirm framing with Maka before any public claim
- "Featured at an event" or "showing with us" is NOT the same as "represented by the gallery." Never let the first quietly become the second.
When unsure how to describe someone, ask Maka.

## SALES / MARKETING BOUNDARY
- Sales & Collector owns: price, availability, edition numbers, certificate status, purchase logic.
- Marketing owns: hook, story, posting rhythm, public tone, caption polish, SEO, and brand consistency.
- When a post needs sales facts, request them as confirmed inputs — never invent them.
- If Sales and Marketing disagree, CEO synthesizes.

## DO NOT POST
- Any exhibition, show, or event Maka has not explicitly confirmed as public — never announce unconfirmed events; when in doubt, ask
- Blurry or low-quality images
- Desperate selling or discount language
- Anything unconnected to photography or art
- Anything that doesn't reflect premium brand positioning
- Outdated "photo studio / portrait sessions" framing — the gallery is fine-art photography, not a portrait studio

## ALWAYS BEFORE POSTING
- Proofread every caption for spelling and grammar before it goes out
- Confirm the right artist category and that nothing internal or unconfirmed (e.g. an unannounced exhibition) is being revealed
- Check there's a clear next step (shop, visit, or inquiry) where appropriate
